## Transcript Memo

Jacklyn's interview reveals a researcher with professional-grade data skepticism — validation is reflexive and non-negotiable, grounded in accountability ("if it has my name on it") rather than distrust of AI specifically. The session's most analytically striking pattern is the volume and specificity of fabrication errors: AI invented a "preference" finding from a usage question, hallucinated external concepts (privacy, user adoption) absent from the data, and surfaced a low-n subgroup as a key finding without flagging it. This is a different failure mode than P01's complaints (vague labels, wrong sentiment) — Jacklyn experienced outright fabrication, which is a more severe validity threat. A notable contrast with P01 emerges on recommendations: P01 valued action recommendations in key findings; Jacklyn explicitly does not want them ("I don't look for actions in those"). Both participants converge on the same gap: AI cannot distinguish what the survey was actually designed to answer (Report Context Gap), though the manifestation differs by use case. The **Interactive Analytics** code is the most distinctive new signal from this session — Jacklyn's model of a useful AI is conversational and queryable, not a static report generator.
