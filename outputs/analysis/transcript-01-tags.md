## Transcript Memo

This session is dominated by a single, recurring evaluative frame: AI insights are useful when they confirm and elevate what the team already knows, and problematic when they ignore how the survey was actually built. The highest-value moments in the interview come from the first call resolution and training recommendations in the Key Findings section — both named existing priorities and gave the participant something she could take to leadership. The lowest-value moments come from the sentiment feature and the open-ended section label, where AI outputs are either actively wrong or stripped of the branching logic that gives them meaning. A notable tension runs throughout: participant has a statistics background that makes her unwilling to trust AI outputs without validation, yet she operates alone and finds validation both time-consuming and uncertain. This suggests that trust in AI is not just a feature of AI accuracy — it is also a function of whether she has the institutional support to verify it.
